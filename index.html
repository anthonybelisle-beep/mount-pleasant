Section 01 — The Foundation

The Property Today

The Mount Pleasant industrial site is an existing, operational property — five acres of freehold industrial land with established road access, secure yard area, and approximately 15,580 square feet of warehouse and office improvements already standing. The project does not begin from raw land. It begins from a working foundation.

Section 03 — Project Features

Six Components. One Campus.

The Mount Pleasant campus is structured as a multi-use operating asset — not a single-purpose facility. Each component draws from a different segment of the local industrial economy.

01

Manufacturing & Steel Fabrication

The campus's anchor production facility — a centralized manufacturing operation producing steel-frame and modular building systems for the regional construction market.

Materials Showroom & Contractor Supply

03

Commercial Rental Bays

Purpose-built leasable bays for contractors, trades, equipment-rental operators, and adjacent industrial businesses serving the local construction economy.

Mini-Storage & Yard Storage

Indoor mini-storage units alongside secure outdoor yard storage — answering a chronic shortage of organized storage capacity in the western corridor.

Section 04 — Location

Mount Pleasant. Cayo District.

Cayo District is western Belize's industrial hub — positioned along the country's primary east-west commercial corridor and serving as the natural staging point for construction, materials distribution, agriculture, and logistics across the central and western regions.

The Mount Pleasant site sits on the George Price Highway corridor, with established road access and proximity to both Belmopan, the country's capital, and the western border crossing into Guatemala. Industrial demand in this corridor is durable and underserved.
